A
Activate Soak and dehydrate nuts and seeds over several hours or days so they are more digestible.
Albumen Egg white.
Antipasto Italian for 'starter'.
Aperitif Alcoholic drink drunk prior to eating dinner, thought to be beneficial to the appetite.
Acidify Make a dish slightly acidic or sharp in taste.
Activating Refers to the method of soaking and dehydrated nuts and seeds, making them more digestible.
Aerate To add air to food or expose a liquid to air.
A la In the style of.
Al dente Refers to vegetables or pasta; after cooking they should retain a little resistance when cut or bitten in to, not too soft.
Aromates Herbs and roots that are full of flavor and aroma.
Aromatics Ingredients that give off the aroma they taste of. Examples include cinnamon, cloves, dill, garlic, star anise, rosemary, shallots.
Au gratin Food baked in the oven in a sauce (often white or cheese sauce) and with breadcrumbs or similar sprinkled on top.
Au jus Serving meat in its own pan juices on the top or on the side.
B
Bain-marie A container placed inside a dish half-filled with simmering water, used to keep food warm and sometimes used to cook delicate foods in the oven without overcooking.
Bake blind The process of baking a pastry first without the filling. The pastry is weighted down with ceramic beads, pastry weights or uncooked beans or rice.
Baking Method of cooking food in an oven by direct heat.
Barbecue Method of cooking foods over wood or charcoal on a grill, on a spit or in an open pit.
Bard To apply fat or tie lardons (thin layers of un-rendered bacon) on top of meat to keep it moist while roasting in the oven.
Baste To spoon, brush or pour liquid over food as it cooks in the oven to moisten and flavor.
Batter A mixture of liquids and flour beaten together to form a semi-liquid for making pancakes, light cakes or for coating meat or vegetables (as in tempura) prior to frying.
Bavarois Flavored creamy custard, sometimes flavored with liquer, that is set with gelatin.
Beard A plant growth attached to the mussel shell that is discarded before cooking.
Beat To mix with a fork, spoon, whisk or electric mixer in order to make a mixture smooth and to incorporate air to make it light and/or fluffy.
Béchamel sauce White sauce made from a white roux and milk.
Béarnaise sauce Made from clarified butter emulsified in egg yolks, with white wine vinegar or lemon juice.
Blackening A Cajun cooking technique.
Buerre blanc A wine-based reduction into which butter is then whisked to thicken and to make into a sauce.
Beurre manié Equal quantities of softened butter and flour mixed together and added to stews, sauces or casseroles a little at a time to thicken.
Bind Adding an ingredient such as an egg to hold other ingredients together.
Binding agent Any ingredient that helps a mixture hold its shape or hold other ingredients together.
Bisque Soups made from shellfish.
Blanch To plunge ingredients into boiling water briefly (to either bring out the color or to loosen the skin) then immediately plunge into icy water to halt the cooking process.
Blend To completely combine ingredients together until smooth.
Boil To cook ingredients in a liquid at boiling point.
Bone To bone is to remove bones from fish, poultry or meat using a thin-bladed knife.
Bone broth Bone broth is a mineral-rich health-boosting stock made by gently simmering animal bones.
Bouillon French for stock.
Bouquet garni A small bundle of herbs (often bay leaves, parsley, thyme or rosemary) and peppercorns tied in muslin and added for flavor to stews or soups.
Braise To simmer meat especially in a tightly-covered dish in a small amount of liquid, to tenderize it.
Bread To crumb fish, chicken or vegetables before frying or deep-frying.
Brine A solution of water, salt, sugar and aromatics used to tenderize and add flavor to meat. Brining is also used to preserve foods such as pickles.
Brioche A light sweet yeast-leavened bread (or roll) enriched with egg yolks.
Broil To grill with the heat source coming from above instead of below (as in barbecueing).
Broth The flavored liquid resulting from ingredients cooked in water.
Brown To cook the outside of food quickly over high heat in order to sear it and seal in its juices.
Bruise To crush aromatics slightly to release their flavor; such as garlic or ginger.
Brunch A mixture of breakfast and lunch, staring around 11am.
Butter To spread a thin film of butter (or oil) onto baking pans or tray to prevent sticking.
Butterfly A technique used to split meat almost in half lengthwise so that it opens like a book; commonly used to speed up cooking and ensure it is cooked evenly all the way through.
C
Canape Toasted (usually) bread shapes topped with a spread and other savory ingredients served as an hors d'oeuvre.
Caramelize To cook ingredients containing natural sugar to a golden brown color.
Casserole A large, deep dish used for cooking food in the oven.
Chef's knife A chef's knife is commonly 8" (20cm) to 14" (36cm) lengths. The metal is ridged, 1 1/2 " (3.8cm) wide and thick at the heel, tapering in width and thickness to the tip.
Chiffonade To finely julienne a vegetable leaf or herb.
Chill Place in a refrigerator or on ice until cold.
Chinois A conical strainer.
Chowder A thick soup often incorporating bacon, seafood, potato and thickened with a roux or crushed crackers or biscuit.
Chop To cut food into pieces, either finely or coarsely.
Ciabatta Italian bread made from wheat flour, salt, water and yeast.
Clarified butter Butter that has had the water and milk solids removed, such as ghee.
Clarify Skim away sediment to turn a cloudy liquid clear, usually by adding egg whites which attract the unwanted particles, then straining them out.
Coat To cover the whole surface of a food with flour, batter or crumbs.
Coddle To poach slowly and gently in simmering water.
Cold pressed Cold pressed oils are the first pressings from the nut and seed, extracted without heat.
Combine To mx together two or more ingredients.
Compote Fruits stewed in sweet syrup.
Concentrated pan juices Reduced pan juices left over after cooking meat in a pan.
Consommé A clear, strong-flavored soup made using stock.
Convection Heat transfer that occurs when the heat is spread by the movement of air, steam or liquid.
Cool To let sit at room temperature until no longer warm.
Compound butter Butter that has been beaten with other ingredients such as herbs, spices or flavorings.
Corned Salted or cured.
Crackling The crisp browned skin of a roast pork, chicken or duck.
Cream Combine together until soft and creamy and resembling the texture of whipped cream.
Crème fraiche A dairy product similar in taste and texture to sour cream.
Crimp Use your fingers to decorate the edge of a pastry crust.
Croute Rounds of crispy fried bread used as a raised base instead of pastry for savory dishes.
Croutons Crisply fried cubes of bread used to garnish soups or as a base for hors d'oeuvres.
Crumb Dredge or coat an ingredient with bread or cereal crumbs or similar.
Cube Cut food into small square pieces.
Cuisson The juices left in a pan or dish after meat, fish or poultry has been cooked.
Curdle Separation that occurs in cream or egg-based mixtures when they are heated too quickly or when an acid is added to a dairy product.
Cure To preserve meats by salting, pickling, drying or smoking.
D
Dash Add a small quantity of liquid, such as 1-2 shakes of soy sauce.
Deep-fry Cook immersed in hot fat or oil until crisp and golden.
Deglaze Add liquid such as wine, vinegar or stock to a hot pan (after the meat has been removed) to turn the resulting juices and browned bits from the pan into a glaze or sauce.
Dehydrate To dry food in order to remove most of its natural moisture so as to preserve it.
Devein To remove the intestinal tract that runs down the back of crustaceans.
Dice Cut ingredients into even-sized cubes.
Dilute Add liquid to a dish to thin it out.
Dissolve Stir a solid food (e.g. sugar) into a liquid until none of the solid remains.
Dollop Approximately a heaped dessertspoonful of semi-firm mixture such as yogurt or sour cream.
Dot To scatter small pieces of butter over the surface of a prepared dish.
Double boiler Two saucepans that fit together; the lower half is filled with water, brought to the boil and left to simmer, creating enough heat to gently melt (chocolate) or cook delicate dishes.
Dredge Coat ingredients in flour, crumbs, cacao or icing sugar.
Dripping The residue fat and juices left in a roasting pan after cooking meat or poultry.
Drizzle To slowly pour in a very thin stream.
Dry ingredients Any ingredient in a recipe that does not contain any moisture; i.e. flour, cacao, sugar, dry spices.
Dry roast A method mainly used to heat whole spices until aromatic before cooling and grinding.
Dust Sprinkle very lightly with an ingredient such as icing sugar or cacao.
E
Egg wash Beaten egg yolks or whites (or both) mixed with milk or water and used to brush over baked goods before and during baking to give them an added sheen.
Emulsify To combine two ingredients together that do not combine easily (e.g. oil and water) slowly while mixing rapidly.
Enchilada A Mexican dish made by filling a tortilla then baking it in a sauce, usually tomato-based.
En papillote A cooking process that completely encloses food to allow foods to cook in their own juices.
Entrée Main course. In Europe it means a dish served between two main courses.
Evaporate Cause liquid to become vapor over heat, leaving a more concentrated residue.
F
Filet A boneless, skinless piece of meat.
Fillet Cut meat or flesh from the bone.
Fines herbes Mixture of herbs such as chervil, chives, parsley and tarragon of French haute cuisine.
Filter Remove impurities by straining through cheesecloth.
Flake Using a fork to lightly break away a small piece of fish flesh to check for doneness.
Flambé Pour warmed brandy or liqueur over food then ignite using a match.
Flute Decorate the edges of a pie crust in a scalloped-shaped pattern.
Fold Gently combine an ingredient into another or into a batter by gently lifting from underneath using a spatula or large spoon so air is not lost from the mixture.
Fondue A food preparation method involving simmering oil or cheese (or chocolate) over a flame to provide a melted coating for various foods.
Fricassée Method of cooking meat pieces that are sautéed and braised in liquid to be made into a sauce.
Frittata Beaten eggs containing other ingredients cooked on the stove top then finished under a grill.
Frizzle Fry julienned vegetables in hot oil until crispy.
Frosting A mixture of icing sugar and a liquid used to ice a cake.
Fry Cook ingredients in hot fat or oil over high heat until brown and crisp.
G
Gammon Uncooked, unsmoked cured bacon; can be used similarly to bacon and ham.
Garnish Decorate a dish with edibles, to make a dish more appealing to the eye.
Gazpacho A Spanish cold soup made from raw vegetables such as tomatoes, onion, garlic and cucumber.
Ghee Clarified butter; almost entirely saturated fat, highly regarded by French and Indian chefs.
Gratin Food such as vegetables topped with grated cheese (and often breadcrumbs) and baked in a shallow dish or browned under a hot grill.
Grill Cook with intense direct heat over charcoal, gas or electricity.
H
Hollow out Remove the middle to leave a space which can be filled.
Homogenized A food, such as milk, that has been processed so the fats will not separate.
Hors d'oeuvre A bite-sized savory, either hot or cold, usually served before dinner with cocktails.
Hull Remove the stem of strawberries.
Husk Remove the outside leaves of an ear of corn.
I
Infuse Steep something in liquid to extract its flavors.
Invert Turn something upside down, such as a cake onto a rack to cool.
J
Jacket The term used to describe a potato still in its skin or unpeeled.
Jell To congeal or become jellylike in consistency
Julienne Cut ingredients such as vegetables into thin matchstick-sized strips.
Jus The natural pan juices left over after cooking a roast.
K
Knead Work dough with the palms and heel of your hands until it is the desired elasticity.
Knock back Punch down dough after it has risen to expel gases before kneading.
L
Lard Rendered pork fat.
Lardons Strips of fat or bacon used to bard a roast; usually removed 30 minutes prior to finishing cooking so that the meat can brown.
Leaven To cause a mixture to rise while baking by adding yeast, baking powder or (bicarbonate) soda.
Line Cover the surface of a pan or baking tray with parchment paper to prevent food sticking to it.
M
Macerate To cover fruits or vegetables with a liquid such as a liqueur or lemon juice and sugar and allow to sit until they soften and absorb the flavors.
Marinade An effective marinade contains seasonings and aromatics for flavor, acid to break down the muscles fibers and collagen and an oil to moisturize the meat and distribute flavors.
Marinate Allow food, (meat especially) to sit in a marinade to tenderize it and give it more flavor.
Mask Cover cooked food with a sauce.
Medallion A very small, round portion of food, used mainly in reference to meat.
Melt Change solids into liquids using slow heat, such as butter, chocolate, cheese.
Mortar A bowl made of marble, stone or hard wood used to pound spices and so on, using a pestle.
Mull Infuse hot wine or cider with herbs and spices.
P
Panfry To fry in a pan using just enough oil to lubricate the pan and over lower heat than does a sauté.
Parboil Boil until partially cooked only.
Pare To peel the skin of a fruit or vegetable.
Pass Force a mixture through a sieve or strainer.
Patisserie French for small cakes or pastries.
Pestle A porcelain or hard wood implement with a rounded blunt end used to pound spices and so on.
Petit four A small cake that is iced on the top and sides and decorated.
Pinch The amount of food, such as salt, that you can hold between your thumb and forefinger.
Pit Remove a pit, seed or stone from fruit.
Poach Cook ingredients gently in a simmering liquid.
Pot roast A slow cooking method using steam.
Puree Process to a thick mixture.
R
Ragout French for stew.
Ramekin Individual high-sided baking dishes.
Reconstitute Dehydrate dried foods by soaking in a liquid from the recipe or hot water.
Reduce Cook liquid over high heat until it has reduced by at least a half in order to improve flavor and consistency and to thicken it.
Reduction A reduction is a liquid usually made from pan juices with some wine or vinegar and herbs added, and reduced in volume to intensify its flavor then made into a sauce.
Refresh Plunge cooked vegetables in icy water to stop the cooking process or help retain their color.
Relax pastry Allow pastry to sit in the fridge for 30 minutes or so that it contracts and does not shrink while baking.
Render Use low heat to make a solid fat liquid.
Reserve Keep an ingredient or mixture from a recipe until later in the cooking process.
Roast Cook meat or vegetables uncovered, by direct heat in the oven or on a spit.
Roux A mixture of butter and flour cooked slowly over low heat then used to thicken sauces, soups and gravies.
S
Sauté Fry quickly in a little hot fat until lightly browned, turning food frequently during cooking.
Scald Heat a liquid until it just starts to bubble around the edges.
Score Make thin shallow slashes along the surface of meat or fish to either tenderize, allow marinade to be absorbed when marinating or allow fat to drain (as in sausages).
Scramble Prepare eggs by adding milk or cream then stirring or whipping with a fork before cooking.
Seal Quickly cook meat over high heat to seal in the juices and retain moisture and flavor.
Sear Brown the surfaces of meat very quickly in a pan or under a grill over high heat to seal in juices.
Season Add flavor to foods to enhance them. Examples are salt, pepper, herbs, spices, lemon juice.
Set Allow food to solidify, as in a jelly.
Sharpen Add an acid such as vinegar or lemon juice to sharpen the flavor of a dish that is dull.
Shell Remove the hard outer covering of nuts, seeds or crustaceans.
Shuck Remove the outer hard shell of an oyster or clam.
Sieve A strainer used to separate solids from liquid.
Sift Pass dry ingredients such as flour and icing sugar through a fine mesh sieve to remove lumps.
Simmer Cook food in liquid over low heat just below boiling point.
Skewer A long metal or wooden pin used to suspend or secure food in shape while cooking.
Skim Spoon fat or scum that rises to the surface of liquid as it cooks.
Slake Mix corn flour or arrowroot with a little liquid before adding to a dish to thicken it.
Sliver A small narrow portion or piece.
Slurry A thickening mixture made up of equal parts flour and water, used to thicken stews and sauces.
Smoke point The temperature at which an oil or fat begins to emit a bluish smoke.
Spongy When a food such as meat or cake has slight resistance and bounces back when touched.
Steam Method of cooking food, separately and covered, over boiling water.
Steep Place dry ingredients (such as tea) in a hot liquid to extract its flavors and color.
Stew Cook meats, beans and vegetables slowly in liquid in a covered pan, often on the stove top.
Stir-fry Cook small pieces of food quickly in a lightly oiled frying pan or wok over high heat. A stir-fry is also the term used to describe a common method of Chinese cooking.
Strain Separate solid foods from a liquid by pouring the mixture through a sieve or strainer.
Stuff Fill a cavity in a food item with an edible, flavorsome mixture of other foods.
Submerge Totally immerse in liquid.
Sweat Cook a small amount of fat over low heat without browning, to release its flavor and moisture.
T
Temper Stabilize a cool ingredient (such as an egg) before adding it gradually to a hot ingredient, or visa versa, to avoid separation and curdling.
Thin To thin out a dish is to add water or stock to dilute it.
Toss Quickly and gently mix ingredients together such as in a salad or pasta dish.
Truss To tie, secure or bind, as in poultry after its been stuffed.
V
Velouté A light velvety sauce using stock that has not been roasted.
Velvet A common Chinese technique used in stir-frying, in which meat is marinated in a mixture of white wine, corn flour and rice wine, blanched in hot water, then stir-fried.
Vinaigrette A dressing made by combine an oil with an acid such as vinegar or lemon juice.
W
Water bath A cooking technique in which a pot of food is cooked in the oven or stove top in a shallow pan of warm water on gentle heat, in order to protect a delicate dish.
Wet ingredients Any liquid called for by a recipe, including eggs, butter, melted chocolate, milk, yogurt, etc.
Whip Beat rapidly in order to add air to an ingredient or increase the volume.
Whisk Beat rapidly in a circular motion.
Woody The part of a fruit or vegetable that is tough or fibrous, such as the base of asparagus.
Work in Add one ingredient to another by beating, kneading or pounding, depending on the recipe.
Z
Zest The outer peel of citrus fruit. To zest is to remove the colored peel of citrus fruit in strips, without the white pith just underneath the surface.
Zester The small utensil used specifically to zest the peel of citrus fruit.
